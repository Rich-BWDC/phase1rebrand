Why We Exist

The data infrastructure around wealth, ownership, and economic mobility in America is fragmented, inaccessible, and politically contested. We're here to fix that.

For decades, policymakers, advocates, journalists, and community leaders have had to make consequential decisions about wealth equity using data that is outdated, incomplete, or locked behind paywalls. The result: programs designed on bad evidence, gaps that persist, and narratives that outrun the facts.

The Center for Wealth Data was founded to change this. We produce and maintain the open data infrastructure, research tools, and translation resources that make the real economics of wealth ‚Äî who has it, who doesn't, and why ‚Äî legible to anyone who needs it.

We are nonpartisan, independent, and committed to open access. We believe that rigorous data in the hands of many is more powerful than proprietary data in the hands of few.

Our Principles

These are the commitments that shape how we work, what we build, and who we build it for.

01

Open data is a public good.

Knowledge concentrated is power concentrated. We open it up.

Wealth data has historically been expensive to produce, slow to publish, and difficult to use. The institutions best positioned to act on it ‚Äî community organizations, local governments, investigative journalists ‚Äî are often the least able to access it.

We make all of our core data and research freely available. No paywalls, no restricted licenses. We publish our methodology, share our code, and welcome scrutiny. We believe that data open to critique is data you can trust.

02

Rigor is not optional.

The stakes of bad data are too high to cut corners on method.

Wealth equity debates are often won or lost on the quality of evidence. Data that is ideologically motivated, methodologically weak, or insufficiently transparent can do real harm ‚Äî reinforcing bad policy, undermining credibility, and eroding public trust.

We hold ourselves to peer-review standards even when publication speed is not required. We are transparent about limitations. We update our datasets as better evidence becomes available, and we are honest when the data does not tell a clean story.

03

Context is data too.

Numbers without narrative mislead. We translate as well as publish.

A county's median household wealth means nothing without understanding its housing market, its demographic history, its access to credit. Raw data, uncontextualized, can distort as easily as it can illuminate.

We invest heavily in policy translation ‚Äî working alongside researchers, practitioners, and communities to make sure our data is interpreted correctly and used well. We produce explainers, methodology guides, and case studies alongside every major data release.

04

The gap is national.

Wealth inequality is not a regional problem. Our scope matches the challenge.

The racial and economic wealth gap is one of the most consequential structural features of American life. It shapes health outcomes, educational attainment, political participation, and intergenerational mobility. It is not a problem confined to particular cities, states, or communities.

We build national data infrastructure because that is the scale of the problem. Our datasets span all 50 states, over 3,200 counties, and reach back across decades ‚Äî giving researchers and policymakers the longitudinal and geographic breadth to understand structural causes, not just surface-level symptoms.

05

Partners multiply impact.

We do not work alone, and we do not intend to.

Data infrastructure, like physical infrastructure, is most valuable when it is shared. Our datasets, tools, and research are designed to be built upon ‚Äî by universities, nonprofits, government agencies, and journalists who can reach communities and decision-makers we cannot.

We actively seek research and institutional partnerships. We offer data licensing for academic use, collaborative research opportunities, and technical integration support for organizations building products or programs on top of our infrastructure.

Who We Are

Researchers, builders,
and translators.
Grounded in evidence.

The Center for Wealth Data is a team of economists, data engineers, policy researchers, and communications professionals united by a conviction that better data produces better outcomes.

We are not an advocacy organization. We take no positions on specific legislation. We do take a firm position that accurate, open, rigorously produced data is a precondition for any meaningful progress on wealth equity ‚Äî and that producing it is a mission worth dedicating a career to.
